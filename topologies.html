Topologies

Introduction

The physical and logical structure of a network (Layout of network).

Definitions

Bus

Also referred to as line topology, each computer is connected to a single cable

Star

One of the most common, every node connects to a central network device such as a hub, switch or computer. The central devices acts as the server and the nodes as clients.

Ring

Devices are connected to each other in a circular shape. Each packet is sent through the ring until it reaches its destination.

Mesh

Each computer is interconnected with each other allowing for most transmissions to be distributed, this topology isn't commonly used.

Hybrid

A type of topology that uses more than one of the above.

Cloud Computing

Introduction

Cloud computing is the use of a network of remote servers hosted on the internet to keep data stored apposed to having it stored locally. However it has it's advantages and disadvantages.

Advantages vs Disadvantages

Advantages:

- Easy to scale up or down the storage amount

- Can access the data from anywhere because it is not limited to one device (locally)

- Your team can access, edit and share documents or data anytime. Able to do more

- Can access your data in the cloud no matter what (eg if you lose your laptop you'll also lose the valuable data on it)

- Donâ€™t have to worry about servers, they are held somewhere else. Suppliers can take care of them and keep them updated

Disadvantages:

- Your data is fully dependable on your internet connection, if its slow or you don't have any it'll make it really hard for you to work

- Your account could be hacked and may lose your valuable data

- You have to rely on third parties to run your IT

- Often the customer is not the only owner of the data on the cloud, needs to be careful

Cloud Services

Google Drive

One of the most popular cloud services is Google drive. Created by google in 2012, since then this service has had many updates adding new and better features. People are able to create and edit doccuments online, as well as share them with other friends or collegues.

iCloud

Another well known service is iCloud, this was created by apple and is mostly used for apples devices, as that's what it's most compatible with. It's able to back up your entire phone or ipad as well as store doccuments from pages, keynote etc.

Dropbox

Dropbox is a file hosting service operated by Dropbox Inc, headquartered in San Francisco. This services was founded in 2007. Dropbox founder Houston developed the Dropbox concept after repeatedly forgetting his USB flash drive while he was a student at MIT.

One Drive

One drive is microsoft's cloud storage, a great fature of this service is being able to connect it to your mac or PC and keep your files synced with the computer. That way you always have a copy even when there is a loss of Wi-Fi, or if you need to access documents from your computer while you don't have it with you.

Mega

The New Zealand-based website was launched in 2013, so it's still fairly new. This service give you 50gb of free storage when you sign up compared to other which usually give 5-15gb.

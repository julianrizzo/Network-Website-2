Network Sizes

Introduction

There are many different peices of hardware used everyday by proffesionals. These include servers, hubs, modems and so on. But, what do they all do? Simple tasks such as sending an email requires wireless access points and the rest of it.

Definitions

LAN

Local Area Network - Interconnects computers within a limited area such as a residence, school, laboratory, or office building.

WAN

Wide Area Network - Covers a relatively large geographical area. Usually, a WAN consists of two or more local-area networks

PAN

Personal Area Network - Used for data transmission amongst devices such as computers, telephones, tablets and personal digital assistants.

MAN

Metropolitan Area Network - Interconnects users with computer resources in a geographic area or region larger than that covered by even a large local area network but smaller than the area covered by a wide area network.

CAN

Controller Area Network - Allow microcontrollers and devices to communicate with each other in applications without a host computer.

WLAN

Wireless Local Area Network - deployed in areas where a wide number of computers may connect to the network but not at the same time. Places like coffee shops often add WLAN to their shops to entice more customers who do not stay for extended periods.
